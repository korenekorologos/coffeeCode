Software Developer vs Software Engineer

  • By Korene Korologos
  • June 19, 2020
  • Fundamentals

Even though Software Developer and Software Engineer may sound the same; there are some fundamental differences between the two that are worth noting.

An Engineer generally will have a formal education that consists of engineering principals, and also a thorough understanding of engineering concepts, in order for them to create solutions.

On the other hand, a Developer tends to be a bit more of a creative mindset. Applying patterns and practices learned through self-discovery, on the job, reading, and tutorials. They are focused on specific aspects of the development lifecycle without the fundamentals of scientific method and engineering principals. Some individuals have formal training while a large amount are self-taught, and arguably just as talented.

So, when looking for a dev or software gig; be clear on what you're applying for!
